# Comb design context

Captured from Jed's direction. Every design pass reads this first. Correct
anything that drifts from what you meant.

## Design Context

### Users

Members of Buzz communities, on iPhone. The first is Jed's `designers`
community: working designers talking shop, sharing critique, posting jobs.
They are design-literate and will notice craft, but they are **not**
Nostr-literate and must never be asked to be. Success is someone joining,
reading, and sending a message without ever learning what a key is.

Context of use: phone in hand, often one-handed, often between other things.
Chat sessions are short, frequent, and interruptible.

### Brand personality

**Warm, exact, unshowy.** Comb sits beside Buzz and should read as part of the
same world without wearing its clothes: same palette, type scale, radii, and
motion, its own mark and name. Not affiliated with Block, and says so.

The voice is plain and human. It says what happened and what to do about it.
It does not apologise, does not exclaim, and never uses jargon where a normal
word exists. "Your account stays on this iPhone," not "your keypair is
persisted locally."

### Aesthetic direction

**Native iOS first, always.** Jed's words: it must "look stunning and look like
a typical iOS app." System components are the default (`Form`, `List`,
`Section`, `TextField`, standard navigation, context menus, `.glass` button
styles). Custom chrome exists only where the product *is* the chrome: the
message timeline, the compose bar, the mark.

This is also the future-proofing requirement: an app built from system
components and semantic text styles inherits each OS release's design
language, including iOS 27, by rebuilding against the new SDK. Hand-drawn
chrome freezes in the year it was written. Hand-built input boxes are
specifically banned; that mistake was made and removed once already.

Theme: **both**, derived honestly. The gradient carries the brand in either
appearance, and a chat app is used in bed at midnight and on a train at noon.

**Anti-references.** Comb should not look like:

- **A generic AI app.** No purple-blue gradients, no glassmorphism as
  decoration, no rounded card with a soft shadow and an icon above every
  heading. If someone could say "an AI made this," that is the failure.
- **A crypto or Nostr-native client.** No keys or hashes on display, no
  neon-on-black, no terminal aesthetic, no protocol jargon in the UI. The
  whole premise is that the protocol stays invisible.
- **Corporate-neutral SaaS.** No all-grey, no absent point of view. It should
  be recognisably this product and not any product.

### Design principles

1. **Native beats bespoke.** If Apple ships a control that does the job, use
   it. Accessibility, Dynamic Type, and next year's design language come free.
2. **Chartreuse is the scarcest resource.** `#D7D700` marks the single most
   important thing on a screen: the primary action, your own reaction, the
   send button. Three uses on one screen means two are wrong.
3. **Never make them learn the protocol.** Key, npub, nsec, seed, and relay do
   not appear anywhere a first-time user can reach. Settings is where the
   vocabulary is allowed, because whoever goes looking brought it with them.
4. **Say the true thing plainly.** Errors name what happened and what to do.
   Privacy claims are exact or absent.
5. **Tune the token, not the call site.** Every visual decision is a token, so
   a design change is a one-file diff and the system cannot drift.
6. **Never grey on colour.** A grey fill or grey text over the gradient reads
   washed out, because the grey fights the hue behind it. Use a luminance
   shift (white or black at low opacity) which preserves hue, or a shade of
   the background itself. Chrome over the gradient blends with `plusLighter`
   in dark and `plusDarker` in light.

### Density

**Comfortable, not compact.** Generous spacing, avatars on every run, built
for reading one message properly rather than scanning fifty. Do not tighten
the timeline without asking.

### Motion

**Push further.** Motion is a place to invest, not ration. The arrival
animation (blur, rise, 500ms on the arrival curve) is the signature; extend
that vocabulary to message arrival, channel-to-timeline transitions via
`glassEffectID`, and staggered reveals. Every motion still honours Reduce
Motion, and still uses the `Motion` tokens rather than ad-hoc timings.

### Accessibility

**WCAG AA, verified on device.** Not best-effort: contrast measured rather
than eyeballed, VoiceOver run end to end, Dynamic Type exercised through the
largest accessibility sizes. Labels and scaling are built in and correct by
construction, but hardware verification is outstanding and should happen
before TestFlight.

## Outstanding

- Contrast has been reasoned about from the Catppuccin values, never measured.
  The muted glyph tones and subtext on the gradient are the likely failures.
- VoiceOver has never been run end to end; labels are correct as written but
  unheard in sequence.
